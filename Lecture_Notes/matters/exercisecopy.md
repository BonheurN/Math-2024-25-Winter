## 1. Basic Operations on Matrices

For the following matrices:

$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$  
$B = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$  
$C = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$  
$D = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$  
$E = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 4 \\ 5 & 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$  

### Calculate:
- $A + B$
- $B - A$
- $A + C$
- $D + E$

### Calculate:
- $\frac{1}{2}A$
- $2B$
- $-3C$
- $4D$

### Calculate the products:
- $A \cdot B$
- $B \cdot A$
- $A \cdot D$
- $D \cdot E$

## 8. Linear Equations by Cramer's Rule

1. Solve:  
   $2x_1 - 3x_2 = 7$  
   $3x_1 + 5x_2 = 2$  

2. Solve:  
   $2x + y - z = 1$  
   $x - y + 2z = 4$  
   $3x - 2z = -1$  

3. Solve:  
   $x + y + z - t = 2$  
   $x - z + 2t = 6$  
   $2x - 3y + t = 4$  
   $3x + y + 3z - 4t = -2$  

4. Why can't the following be solved using Cramer's rule?  
   $x_1 + 2x_2 + 3x_3 = 3$  
   $4x_1 + 5x_2 + 6x_3 = 2$  
   $7x_1 + 8x_2 + 9x_3 = 1$
